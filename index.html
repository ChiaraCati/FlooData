Project

The project's goal is to make useful use of open data from idrogeology related to the Emilia-Romagna region of Italy. Our initial goal is to examine any connections between idrogeological harms and the actions taken by various players to address them at the local, national, and (perhaps) international levels. Then, damages were separated into two groups:

  • Damages on the coast (mostly damages caused by sea events)
  • Damages on the mainland (damages cused by floods and earth-quakes)

This distinction was made when we elaborated on our second goal, which was to see whether there is a difference in how these two unique sectors are perceived as far as risk goes. Information on damages has now been combined with data on various initiative, primarily differentiating between preventive and interventional ones. This additional distinction was developed in an effort to be clear about the level of local and larger-scale institutions' (preferably private and public) contribution to the preservation of this area.
