Project

As one of the widest Italian regions, Emilia-Romagna, is characterized by a very diversified hydrogeological conformation: including coastal areas, mountains, hills, and still there is space for a vast lowland area, which is covered by waterway. But high diveristy in ecosystems also implies a wide variety of calamities and disasters, hence an high degree of attention, multidisciplinary approaches and constant investments in order to deal with these kind of events and avoid damagest to regional economy and ecosystems.

  • 25,1% mountains
  • 27,1% hills
  • 47,8% lowland

information taken from Sapere.it

Hydrogeological dissest is related to climate change: the increasement of events like landslides and coast erosion may be considered syntomphs of the bad health codition of our planet, due to human activities such as deforestation, cementification, industrialization, etc. Thus we believe it is important to share this information with citizens to inform them about the actual risk of the area in which they live or are linked with.

The other side of the coin is represented by the actions undertaken by local communities to avoid and repair this kind of disasters. Here we chose to highlight the diffence in financings devolved for reparatory and preventive activities in order to higlight which areas prefer or are able). Information on damages has now been combined with data on various initiative, primarily differentiating between preventive and interventional ones.

FlooData is a web application that makes re-use of Open Data related to Emilia Romagna's hydrogeological dissest. All of this data has been gathered from 9 different sources having as themes:

  • Geo-data
  • Hydrogeological disasters
  • Human action todeal with this kind of disasters

Which types of hydrogeological disaster do we find in Emilia Romagna, and what is their distribution on this territory?

Map of Emilia Romagna, divided into provinces, with bubbles pointing to disruptions' locations.

Sunburst chart of total amount of disasters divided by province and then divided by typology.


Here we notice the provinces of Bologna and Modena to be the ones with most dissests recorded, some – e.g., wells of hot water and gas leaks from the ground – are linked to the 2012 earthquake that stroke the region (cfr. Segnalazioni fenomeni geologici particolari).
Landslides and idraulic disasters are largely present in each province, also we can assess there is a clear difference in the typology of the disasters between provinces facing on the Adriatic sea. Ferrara is a case worth of mention, as it is one of the provinces registering less disasters, but at the same time having an etherogeneous distribution of disaster typologies.

From the barchart we can immediatly state that all Provinces are doing pretty well on the preventive matter, Bologna and Modena keep placing themselves on top of rankings as the two cities that also invest the most in prevention. This factor might have also impacted the limited amount of reparation costs compared, when compaaring them to other provinces spending less on prevention – e.g., Parma.
As we ended up with a list of more than 290 types of activities, some of them not strictly related with hydrogeological dissest, we select here 15 categories for dividing the financings. Others is a general category containing all those activities that are linked to hydrogeological dissest, but do not represent active interventions on the territory. Of the remaining categories we notice that direct activities dedicated to landslides and floodings, along with waterways are the most financed ones.
